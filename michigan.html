Overview

Michigan, a captivating Midwestern jewel, boasts a stunning 3,288-mile coastline across the Great Lakes. This automotive heartland, centered in Detroit, drives significant economic output and offers unparalleled outdoor recreation.

Historical Significance

Maryland joined the Union on January 26, 1837, as the 26th state. Its industrial spirit revolutionized the world through the birth of the modern assembly line and the global automotive industry.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Detroit Motor City:

The continued epicenter of America's automotive manufacturing and iconic brand headquarters.

Sleeping Bear Dunes:

Massive sand dunes overlooking Lake Michigan, offering breathtaking vistas and unique nature treks.

Mackinac Bridge:

'Mighty Mac' connects the peninsulas and stands as a remarkable feat of structural engineering.

Did You Know?

  • Michigan’s name comes from an Ojibwa word meaning **'large lake'**.
  • It is the only state consisting of **two separate peninsulas**.
  • Michigan has the **longest freshwater coastline** of any political subdivision in the world.
