Overview

Nevada, the 'Silver State,' is a mesmerizing landscape of Mojave Desert beauty and neon-lit cities. While it's known globally as the entertainment capital of the world, it also holds a profound silver mining legacy and vast mountain terrains.

Historical Significance

Joining the Union on October 31, 1864, during the height of the Civil War, Nevada was admitted as the 36th state. Its 'Battle Born' motto reflects its strategic role in contributing silver to the national treasury.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Las Vegas Strip:

An iconic 4.2-mile boulevard of neon lights and architectural wonders that defines global travel and spectacle.

Hoover Dam:

A staggering engineering feat on the Colorado River that provides critical power and water to millions across the Southwest.

Lake Tahoe:

A majestic alpine lake shared with California, offering crystal clear waters and world-class ski resorts in the Sierra Nevadas.

Did You Know?

  • Nevada contains **more mountain ranges** than any other state in the continental US.
  • The state is home to **Area 51**, the mysterious and highly classified Air Force facility.
  • Nearly **85% of Nevada's land** is managed by the federal government, the highest percentage in the country.
