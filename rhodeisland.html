Overview

Rhode Island, the 'Ocean State,' is the nation's smallest state by area but holds a massive historical presence. With over 400 miles of coastline, it is a premier destination for sailing, maritime research, and exquisite colonial charm.

Historical Significance

Rhode Island joined the Union on May 29, 1790, as the 13th state. It was the first colony to declare independence from Britain and the last of the original 13 to ratify the Constitution, prioritizing religious freedom and individual rights.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Newport Mansions:

Iconic Gilded Age 'summer cottages' built by industrial titans, representing the height of 19th-century American luxury.

The Cliff Walk:

A spectacular public walkway in Newport that offers breathtaking views of the Atlantic Ocean and historic architectural masterpieces.

Brown University:

A prestigious Ivy League research institution in Providence that drives innovation and academic excellence in the region.

Did You Know?

  • Rhode Island is home to **over 30 islands**, despite being mostly part of the mainland.
  • The **Flying Horse Carousel** in Watch Hill, operating since 1884, is the oldest in the country.
  • Rhode Island was the **first colony** to declare independence, doing so two months before the national declaration.
