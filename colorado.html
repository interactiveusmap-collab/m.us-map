Overview

Colorado, the "Centennial State," is a majestic Western U.S. state defined by soaring Rocky Mountain peaks and expansive high plains. Renowned for its world-class skiing, mountain biking, and vibrant outdoor culture, it is a leading center for aerospace technology, renewable energy, and craft brewing.

Historical Significance

Colorado officially joined the Union on August 1, 1876, exactly 100 years after the signing of the Declaration of Independence. Its early history was defined by the drama of the gold rush and the rise of mining empires, transforming it into a pivotal transport hub.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Rocky Mountain National Park:

One of the nation's most visited parks, featuring over 350 miles of spectacular alpine trails.

Red Rocks Amphitheatre:

A world-famous open-air music venue naturally carved out of prehistoric sandstone monoliths.

Aerospace Sector:

Colorado has the nation's second-largest aerospace economy, serving as a hub for satellite manufacturing.

Did You Know?

  • Colorado has more than 50 mountain peaks that exceed 14,000 feet, known as "Fourteeners."
  • The sun shines for over 300 days a year in Denver, more than Miami or San Diego.
  • The cheeseburger was reportedly trademarked in Denver in 1935 at the Humpty Dumpty Drive-In.
