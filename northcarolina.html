Overview

North Carolina, 'First in Flight,' boasts a diverse geography from the Blue Ridge Mountains to the Outer Banks. Home to the globally significant Research Triangle Park, it is a leading hub for technological innovation, advanced healthcare, and elite academic pursuit.

Historical Significance

Joining the Union on November 21, 1789, as the 12th state, North Carolina played a pivotal role in the nation's formation, contributing to early economic stability and the establishment of enduring democratic principles.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Biltmore Estate:

America's largest privately owned house, a Gilded Age masterpiece nestled in the scenic Blue Ridge Mountains.

Wright Brothers Memorial:

The historic site in Kitty Hawk where Wilbur and Orville Wright achieved the first powered flight in 1903.

Outer Banks:

A 200-mile string of barrier islands known for its wild horses, historic lighthouses, and legendary maritime heritage.

Did You Know?

  • North Carolina is the **leading producer** of sweet potatoes in the United States.
  • Pepsi-Cola was **invented in New Bern** by pharmacist Caleb Bradham in 1893.
  • The University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill was the **first public university** to open its doors.
