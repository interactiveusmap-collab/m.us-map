Overview

New Hampshire is defined by the majestic White Mountains and a historical commitment to personal freedom. From its role as the first-in-the-nation primary state to its thriving tech hubs in historic millyards, it perfectly blends colonial heritage with modern innovation.

Historical Significance

Ratifying the Constitution on June 21, 1788, as the 9th state, New Hampshire provided the crucial final vote needed to establish the United States government, forever cementing its status as a foundational pillar of the Republic.

Top Cities & Hubs

Manchester

The largest city in the state, transformed from a historic textile giant into a premier center for high tech and healthcare.

2

Nashua

Population: ~91,000

Twice named the 'Best Place to Live in America,' this southern city is a major retail hub with a strong electronics sector.

3

Concord

Population: ~44,000

The state capital, known for its gold-domed State House and as a center for legal, political, and cultural activities.

4

Derry

Population: ~34,000

A historic town in the Merrimack Valley, famous for its connections to nature through the home of poet Robert Frost.

5

Dover

Population: ~32,000

The oldest permanent settlement in the state, today it is a fast-growing coastal city known for its historic riverfront mills.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Mount Washington:

The highest peak in the Northeast, legendary for its record-breaking winds and breathtaking alpine vistas.

Lake Winnipesaukee:

The state's largest body of water, offering a premier four-season destination for recreation and scenic boating.

Portsmouth Seacoast:

A historic port city renowned for its charming colonial architecture, busy shipyard, and vibrant coastal culture.

Did You Know?

  • New Hampshire was the **first colony** to declare independence from Great Britain.
  • The state has **no general sales tax** and no personal state income tax.
  • Mount Washington once recorded a **231 mph surface wind gust**, the fastest ever measured by humans at the time.
