Overview

North Dakota is a land of dramatic expansive prairies and the rugged, ancient beauty of the Badlands. As a pioneering force in the Bakken oil formation and a leading agricultural giant, it stands as a vital pillar of American energy and food security.

Historical Significance

North Dakota joined the Union on November 2, 1889, as the 39th state. Its admission marked a significant westward expansion, integrating a region of immense natural resources and strategic importance into the growing fabric of the nation.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Theodore Roosevelt National Park:

A breathtaking landscape of painted canyons and diverse wildlife, preserving the rugged soul of the American West.

International Peace Garden:

A 3.6-square-mile park straddling the US-Canada border, symbolizing more than 200 years of peaceful cooperation.

Bakken Oil Formation:

One of the largest oil deposits in the United States, driving a massive energy boom and national independence.

Did You Know?

  • North Dakota is the **leading producer** of sunflowers and honey in the entire United States.
  • The state contains the **geographic center** of the North American continent in the town of Rugby.
  • North Dakota has **more national wildlife refuges** than any other state in the country.
