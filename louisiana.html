Overview

Louisiana, the "Pelican State," is a mesmerizing cultural melting pot where French, Spanish, African, and Native American heritages collide. Famed for its unique Creole soul and Cajun heart, it's a global leader in energy and culture.

Historical Significance

Louisiana officially joined the Union on April 30, 1812, as the 18th state. As the crown jewel of the 1803 Louisiana Purchase, it served as the pivotal gateway to the Mississippi River.

Key Landmarks & Economy

French Quarter:

The oldest neighborhood in New Orleans, a famous National Historic Landmark defined by iron balconies and nightlife.

Jean Lafitte Preserve:

A massive National Historical Park protecting the state's iconic bayous, swamps, and marshes.

Energy & Port Titan:

Louisiana is a massive global producer of natural gas and home to the крупнейший tonnage port in the Western Hemisphere.

Did You Know?

  • Louisiana legal system is derived from the Napoleonic Code of France, unique in the US.
  • Home to world's longest bridge over water, the Lake Pontchartrain Causeway (23.8 miles).
  • Instead of counties, Louisiana is divided into parishes, reflecting its French and Spanish colonial roots.
