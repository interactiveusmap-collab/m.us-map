Overview

Hawaii, the "Aloha State," is a mesmerizing tropical archipelago in the central Pacific. As the only island state in the U.S., it is famed for its breathtaking volcanic landscapes, vibrant indigenous culture, and its status as a global leader in marine research and tourism.

Historical Significance

Hawaii officially joined the Union on August 21, 1959, as the 50th and final state. Its history spans from a sovereign kingdom to the site of the pivotal 1941 Pearl Harbor attack, marking its central role in Pacific history and American presence.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Pearl Harbor:

A National Historic Landmark of immense global significance, home to the USS Arizona Memorial.

Hawaii Volcanoes:

A National Park covering two of the world's most active volcanoes, Kilauea and Mauna Loa.

Tropical Agriculture:

Hawaii is a leading global producer of macadamia nuts, Kona coffee, and exotic tropical flowers.

Did You Know?

  • Hawaii is the only U.S. state that grows coffee commercially in its rich volcanic soil.
  • The state is home to the world's most active volcano, Kilauea, which has erupted almost continuously since 1983.
  • Hawaii does not observe Daylight Saving Time and has its own unique time zone.
