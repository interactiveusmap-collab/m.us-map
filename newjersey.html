Overview

New Jersey, the 'Garden State,' is a powerhouse of pharmaceutical innovation and a vital crossroads for the Northeast. Dense metropolitan hubs perfectly blend with flourishing agricultural traditions and iconic coastal boardalks, creating a unique and diverse landscape.

Historical Significance

Entering the Union on December 18, 1787, as the 3rd state, New Jersey earned its reputation as the 'Crossroads of the Revolution,' hosting more major battles than any other colony during the war for independence.

Top Cities & Hubs

1

Newark

Population: ~311,000

The state's largest city and a global logistics giant, home to one of the nation's busiest airports and major insurance firms.

2

Jersey City

Population: ~292,000

'Wall Street West'—a major financial hub located directly across from Manhattan, known for its skyline and diversity.

3

Paterson

Population: ~159,000

The 'Silk City,' famous for its dramatic Great Falls and its role as the cradle of the American Industrial Revolution.

4

Elizabeth

Population: ~137,000

A premier transportation and retail center, featuring Port Newark-Elizabeth, one of the world's most active container ports.

5

Trenton

Population: ~90,000

The state capital, the historic site of Washington's daring Delaware crossing and a foundational center for law and politics.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Princeton University:

A world-renowned Ivy League institution serving as a beacon of academic excellence and historic architectural beauty.

Atlantic City:

Featuring the world's first boardwalk, this iconic coastal city is a legendary center for gaming, tourism, and nightlife.

Statue of Liberty Park:

Liberty State Park offers panoramic views of the statue and Ellis Island, symbolizing hope and the American immigrant story.

Did You Know?

  • New Jersey has the **highest population density** of any US state.
  • The first **professional basketball game** was played in Trenton in 1896.
  • Atlantic City's streets served as the inspiration for the original **Monopoly** board game.
