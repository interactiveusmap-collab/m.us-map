Overview

Pennsylvania, the 'Keystone State,' is a historic powerhouse in the Northeast. From the 'Steel City' of Pittsburgh to the cobblestone streets of Philadelphia, it serves as a vital bridge between the Atlantic coast and the American Heartland.

Historical Significance

Pennsylvania officially joined the Union on December 12, 1787, as the 2nd state. As the birthplace of American democracy, it hosted the signing of the Declaration of Independence and the US Constitution in Philadelphia.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Independence Hall:

A UNESCO World Heritage site where both the Declaration of Independence and the US Constitution were adopted.

Hershey's Chocolate World:

Located in Hershey, this landmark honors the state's role as a major hub for food manufacturing and confections.

Gettysburg National Military Park:

A hallowed site preserving the battlefield where the Union army halted the Confederate invasion in 1863.

Did You Know?

  • The **Philadelphia Zoo**, opened in 1874, was the first public zoo in the United States.
  • Pennsylvania is known as the **'Snack Food Capital of the World,'** producing 80% of US pretzels.
  • The **Liberty Bell** originally cracked during its first test ring in 1752 and has been a global symbol ever since.
