Overview

Maryland, the 'Old Line State,' is a tapestry of maritime history along the Chesapeake Bay. Renowned for its seafood, especially blue crabs, it thrives as a hub for biotechnology, defense, and high-tech innovation.

Historical Significance

Maryland joined the Union on April 28, 1788, as the 7th state. Its strategic location and commitment to religious freedom played a foundational role in shaping American democratic principles.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Fort McHenry:

The historic bastion whose defense inspired 'The Star-Spangled Banner' during the War of 1812.

Chesapeake Bay:

A massive estuary that defines the state's geography and provides its world-famous seafood bounty.

Inner Harbor:

Baltimore's revitalized waterfront, a major center for tourism, museum-going, and regional economy.

Did You Know?

  • Home to the US’ **oldest seafood market**, dating back to 1792.
  • The nickname **'Old Line'** comes from courageous soldiers in the Revolutionary War.
  • Maryland is called **'America in Miniature'** because it has almost all types of natural terrain.
