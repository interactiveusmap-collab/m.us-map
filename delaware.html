Overview

Delaware, proudly known as the "First State," is a coastal Mid-Atlantic powerhouse that punches far above its weight. Despite its small size, it is a global epicenter for corporate law and finance, attracting half of all Fortune 500 companies with its business-friendly environment.

Historical Significance

Delaware officially joined the Union on December 7, 1787, as the very first state to ratify the U.S. Constitution. Its legacy includes strategic Revolutionary importance and early Dutch and Swedish settlements along the Atlantic coast.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Rehoboth Beach:

Known as the "Nation's Summer Capital," featuring a classic boardwalk and premier Atlantic coastline.

Winterthur Museum:

A world-class estate founded by the du Pont family, housing significant American decorative arts collections.

Corporate Law Hub:

Legal home for over 1.5 million businesses due to its Court of Chancery, the nation's premier business court.

Did You Know?

  • Delaware is famously tax-free for shopping, a major draw for retail tourists.
  • The state is so small it only has three counties, the fewest of any U.S. state.
  • The first beauty contest in the U.S. was reportedly held in Rehoboth Beach in 1880.
