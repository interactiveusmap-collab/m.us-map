Overview

Virginia, the 'Old Dominion,' is a state of profound historical importance and geographic diversity. Stretching from the Chesapeake Bay to the Blue Ridge Mountains, it is a major economic engine driven by federal agencies, military might, and tech innovation.

Historical Significance

Virginia officially joined the Union on June 25, 1788, as the 10th state. Known as the 'Mother of Presidents,' it was the site of the first permanent English settlement at Jamestown in 1607 and served as the primary theater of the Civil War.

Key Landmarks & Economy

The Pentagon:

Located in Arlington, this is the headquarters of the US Department of Defense and one of the world's most recognizable symbols of military power.

Monticello:

The iconic home of Thomas Jefferson, designed by the 3rd US President himself, illustrating the state's rich architectural and political heritage.

Colonial Williamsburg:

The world's largest living history museum, offering a vivid recreation of 18th-century life and Virginia's path to the American Revolution.

Did You Know?

  • Virginia is the **birthplace of more US Presidents** than any other state (total of eight).
  • **70% of the world's daily internet traffic** passes through data centers located in Northern Virginia.
  • The **Chesapeake Bay Bridge-Tunnel** is one of the world's few bridge-tunnel complexes, spanning 17.6 miles.
