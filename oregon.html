Overview

Oregon is a Pacific Northwest jewel, celebrated for its dramatic coastline, dense ancient forests, and forward-thinking urban centers. A world leader in sustainability and outdoor culture, it perfectly balances rugged natural wonders with a flourishing high-tech 'Silicon Forest' economy.

Historical Significance

Oregon officially joined the Union on February 14, 1859, as the 33rd state. Its admission crowned decades of pioneer exploration along the historic Oregon Trail and cemented the United States' claim to the Pacific Northwest.

Top Cities & Hubs

1

Portland

Population: ~650,000

The state's largest city, globally famous for its bridges, bicycle-friendly streets, and its uniquely vibrant 'Keep Portland Weird' culture.

2

Salem

Population: ~175,000

The state capital, nestled in the fertile Willamette Valley and recognized for its political heritage and stunning public gardens.

3

Eugene

Population: ~175,000

Home to the University of Oregon and known as 'Track Town USA,' serving as a major hub for sports, arts, and outdoor enthusiasts.

4

Gresham

Population: ~115,000

A growing residential and commercial city located at the gateway to the majestic Columbia River Gorge and Mount Hood.

5

Hillsboro

Population: ~105,000

The heart of the 'Silicon Forest,' serving as a global center for high-tech manufacturing and semiconductor research.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Crater Lake:

The deepest lake in the US, famous for its crystal-clear blue water and dramatic position within a collapsed volcanic caldera.

Mount Hood:

An iconic, snow-capped stratovolcano that serves as a year-round playground for skiers and mountain climbers.

Nike World Headquarters:

Located in Beaverton, this massive campus drives the global sports apparel and footwear industry.

Did You Know?

  • Oregon is home to the **world's smallest park**, Mill Ends Park, which is only 2 feet across.
  • It is the only state in the US with a **two-sided flag**, featuring a beaver on the reverse.
  • Oregon has **more ghost towns** than any other state in the country, relics of the 19th-century mining gold rushes.
