Overview

Indiana, the "Crossroads of America," is a quintessential Midwestern state where industrial might meets rolling farmland. Best known as the global home of high-speed auto racing and for its legendary "Hoosier Hospitality," it is a leading center for manufacturing and life sciences today.

Historical Significance

Indiana officially joined the Union on December 11, 1816, as the 19th state. It served as a vital frontier gateway, playing a critical role in the abolitionist movement through the Underground Railroad and emerging as an industrial engine for the nation's expansion.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Indy Motor Speedway:

The world's largest spectator sporting facility and home to the world-famous Indianapolis 500.

Indiana Dunes:

A stunning National Park on Lake Michigan featuring massive sand dunes and diverse wetlands.

Manufacturing Hub:

Indiana has the nation's highest concentration of manufacturing jobs while being a top producer of corn.

Did You Know?

  • The first professional baseball game was reportedly played in Fort Wayne, Indiana, in 1871.
  • The city of Warsaw is known as the "Orthopedic Capital of the World," producing ~20% of global orthodontic appliances.
  • Santa Claus, Indiana, is the only post office in the world with that name, receiving thousands of letters annually.
