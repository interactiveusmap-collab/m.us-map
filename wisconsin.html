Overview

Wisconsin is a Midwestern anchor famous for its diverse landscapes, from the fertile 'America's Dairyland' farms to the majestic Great Lakes shores. It is a global leader in agriculture and manufacturing, offering a rich cultural heritage and legendary outdoor recreation.

Historical Significance

Wisconsin entered the Union on May 29, 1848, as the 30th state. It became the heart of the United States' progressive political movement and revolutionized the dairy industry, while its earlier history was defined by the fur trade and crucial lead mining activities.

Top Cities & Hubs

1

Milwaukee

Population: ~580,000

The state's largest city and a historic brewing giant, famous for its cultural festivals, stunning Lake Michigan waterfront, and the world-champion Bucks.

2

Madison

Population: ~270,000

The state capital and home to UW-Madison, a vibrant intellectual and political hub nestled beautifully between four scenic lakes.

3

Green Bay

Population: ~107,000

World-famous 'Titletown,' the smallest city to host an NFL franchise—the Packers—and a primary industrial shipping port on the Great Lakes.

4

Kenosha

Population: ~100,000

A rapidly growing city on the corridor between Chicago and Milwaukee, known for its historic lakefront and strong manufacturing base.

5

Racine

Population: ~77,000

Celebrated for its rich architectural heritage, including landmark Frank Lloyd Wright designs, and its legacy of global industrial innovation.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Wisconsin Dells:

The 'Waterpark Capital of the World,' featuring dramatic glacier-carved sandstone gorges and world-class theme parks.

Lambeau Field:

One of the most historic and revered professional sports stadiums globally, the hallowed home of the Green Bay Packers since 1957.

Dairy Industry:

Wisconsin produces about one-fourth of all US cheese, supporting over 1,200 licensed cheesemakers and a massive agricultural economy.

Did You Know?

  • Wisconsin has over **15,000 inland lakes**, offering more miles of shoreline than California.
  • The **Republican Party was founded** in a small schoolhouse in Ripon, Wisconsin, in 1854.
  • The first **ice milk sundae** was reportedly created in Two Rivers, Wisconsin, in 1881.
