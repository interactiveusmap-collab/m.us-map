Overview

Massachusetts, a cornerstone of history, innovation, and education. Home to prestigious Ivy League universities and a burgeoning biotech industry, it lead the way in science, medicine, and democratic progress for the nation.

Historical Significance

Joined the Union on February 6, 1788, as the 6th state. It was the launching pad for the Revolutionary War and remains a global leader in intellectual pursuit and democratic character.

Did You Know?

  • The **first public school** in America, Boston Latin School, opened in 1635.
  • Basketball was **invented in Springfield** by James Naismith in 1891.
  • Massachusetts was the **first state** to ratify the US Constitution.
