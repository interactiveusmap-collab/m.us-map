Overview

Mississippi, the 'Magnolia State,' is anchored by the mighty Mississippi River. The fertile Delta region famously birthed Blues music, while the state remains a leader in agriculture, timber, and shipbuilding.

Historical Significance

Joining the Union on December 10, 1817, as the 20th state, Mississippi solidified westward expansion and played a central role in the agricultural and political development of the American South.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Vicksburg National Park:

Preserves the pivotal 1863 battlefield that changed the course of American history on the river banks.

Mississippi Capitol:

A magnificent Jackson landmark and architectural masterpiece serving as the seat of state law and heritage.

Natchez Trace Parkway:

A scenic historic drive that follows ancient trade paths through lush forests and significant archeological sites.

Did You Know?

  • Mississippi is the **birthplace of the Blues**, specifically in the Delta region.
  • It produces more **farm-raised catfish** than any other state in the nation.
  • The first **human lung transplant** was performed at the University of Mississippi Medical Center.
