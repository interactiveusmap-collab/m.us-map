Overview

Wyoming is a vast and sparsely populated Western state, celebrated for its extraordinary natural geography and rugged spirit. Home to the world's first national park, it is a global leader in energy production and a premier destination for high-altitude outdoor adventure.

Historical Significance

Wyoming entered the Union on July 10, 1890, as the 44th state. It famously earned the moniker 'The Equality State' for leading the nation in civil rights, becoming the first US territory and later the first state to grant women the right to vote.

Top Cities & Hubs

1

Cheyenne

Population: ~65,000

The state capital and cultural anchor, known for its deep Western heritage and home to Cheyenne Frontier Days—the world's largest outdoor rodeo.

2

Casper

Population: ~58,000

Known as 'The Oil City,' Casper is a primary engine for Wyoming's energy sector and the gateway to the world-renowned North Platte River.

3

Laramie

Population: ~32,000

Home to the University of Wyoming, the state's sole public four-year institution, and a vibrant center for education in the high plains.

4

Gillette

Population: ~33,000

The 'Energy Capital of the Nation,' producing a massive portion of American coal and serving as a critical economic hub for the surrounding region.

5

Rock Springs

Population: ~23,000

A historic mining and cattle town, celebrated for its diverse multi-cultural heritage and its proximity to the beautiful Flaming Gorge.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Yellowstone National Park:

The world's first national park, containing nearly half of the planet's active geysers, including the iconic and reliable Old Faithful.

Grand Teton:

A global alpine destination for climbers and hikers, featuring the dramatic Teton Range and the pristine Snake River ecosystem.

Devils Tower:

The United States' very first National Monument, established in 1906, an 867-foot monolith of igneous rock that holds sacred significance.

Did You Know?

  • Wyoming is the **least populous state** in the US, despite being the 10th largest by total land area.
  • The state was the **first to grant women the right to vote** in 1869, well before the 19th Amendment.
  • Wyoming is home to the **Buffalo Bill Center of the West** in Cody, often called the 'Smithsonian of the West.'
