Overview

Georgia, the celebrated "Peach State," is the dynamic heartbeat of the American Southeast. From the Blue Ridge Mountains to the historic Atlantic coast, it balances agricultural traditions with global leadership in film, aerospace, and logistics.

Historical Significance

Georgia officially joined the Union on January 2, 1788, as the 4th state. It became a crucial battleground in the Revolution and later transformed into the cradle of the Modern Civil Rights Movement, forever linked to the legacy of Dr. Martin Luther King Jr.

5

Savannah

Population: ~150,000

A mesmerizing coastal city defined by historic cobblestone squares and one of the largest shipping ports on the Atlantic.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Centennial Olympic Park:

A cornerstone of Atlanta's downtown legacy, originally built for the 1996 Summer Olympics.

Savannah Historic District:

One of the largest National Historic Landmark districts in the U.S., featuring 22 picturesque park squares.

Hollywood of the South:

Georgia is a global leader in film and TV production, offering massive soundstages for major studios.

Did You Know?

  • Georgia is the only state where both the Vidalia Onion and the peach are official state symbols.
  • The Cumberland Island National Seashore features a herd of wild horses that live on remote beaches.
  • The world-famous soft drink Coca-Cola was invented in Atlanta in 1886 by pharmacist John Pemberton.
