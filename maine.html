Overview

Maine, the 'Pine Tree State,' boasts a rugged coastline punctuated by iconic lighthouses and expansive sandy beaches. Renowned for lobster and timber, it offers breathtaking beauty alongside thriving maritime industries.

Historical Significance

Maine officially joined the United States on March 15, 1820, as the 23rd state. Its admittance expanded the country’s territory and solidified New England's presence following the War of 1812.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Acadia National Park:

Located on Mount Desert Island, this park offers stunning granite peaks and dramatic ocean views.

Portland Head Light:

Perched dramatically on Cape Elizabeth, this iconic lighthouse offers picturesque views and historical depth.

Maritime Excellence:

Maine leads in lobster production and timber, maintaining a legacy of maritime and forestry excellence.

Did You Know?

  • The iconic lobster roll originated in Maine, showcasing the state's rich culinary bounty.
  • Maine has more coastline than all other US states combined, providing a spectacular geography.
