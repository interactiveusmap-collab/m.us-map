Overview

The United States is divided into four major geographic regions by the Census Bureau. Tap any region below to explore.

Northeast Midwest South West

🏛️ Northeast

The cradle of American democracy. Home to the nation's oldest cities, world-class universities, and a powerhouse economy stretching from Wall Street to the Ivy League.

9 States:
Connecticut Maine Massachusetts New Hampshire New Jersey New York Pennsylvania Rhode Island Vermont

🌾 Midwest

America's heartland. A vast agricultural and industrial powerhouse featuring the Great Lakes, sweeping prairies, and iconic cities like Chicago and Detroit.

12 States:
Illinois Indiana Iowa Kansas Michigan Minnesota Missouri Nebraska North Dakota Ohio South Dakota Wisconsin

🌴 South

The largest region by population and area. Rich in history, culture, and natural beauty — from Florida's beaches to Texas's vast plains and the Blue Ridge Mountains.

17 States:
Alabama Arkansas Delaware Florida Georgia Kentucky Louisiana Maryland Mississippi North Carolina Oklahoma South Carolina Tennessee Texas Virginia West Virginia D.C.

🏔️ West

The frontier spirit. Spanning from the Rocky Mountains to the Pacific, it's home to the nation's most dramatic landscapes, tech innovation, and America's last frontier — Alaska.

13 States:
Alaska Arizona California Colorado Hawaii Idaho Montana Nevada New Mexico Oregon Utah Washington Wyoming
