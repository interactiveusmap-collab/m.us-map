Overview

South Carolina, the 'Palmetto State,' is a Southeastern gem defined by its rich history, subtropical climate, and stunning Atlantic coastline. From historic Charleston to the Blue Ridge Mountains, it offers a blend of Southern hospitality and industrial vitality.

Historical Significance

South Carolina joined the Union on May 23, 1788, as the 8th state. As one of the original 13 colonies, it played a pivotal role in the American Revolution and was the site of the first battle of the Civil War at Fort Sumter.

Top Cities & Hubs

1

Charleston

Population: ~150,000

The oldest and largest city, renowned for its antebellum architecture, cobblestone streets, and world-class culinary and hospitality scene.

2

Columbia

Population: ~137,000

The state capital and home to the University of South Carolina, serving as the primary hub for government, education, and the arts.

3

North Charleston

Population: ~117,000

A thriving industrial center hosting a major Boeing assembly facility and serving as a key regional hub for trade and manufacturing.

4

Mount Pleasant

Population: ~95,000

A prestigious community across the Cooper River offering a high quality of life, historic districts, and proximity to beautiful coastal parks.

5

Greenville

Population: ~70,000

A rapidly growing city in the Upstate region, famous for its award-winning downtown, Falls Park, and diverse corporate economy.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Fort Sumter:

The historic sea fort in Charleston Harbor where the first shots of the American Civil War were fired in 1861.

Charleston Historic District:

A beautifully preserved district featuring architectural treasures and lush gardens that tell the story of the state's maritime past.

Boeing & BMW Plants:

Global manufacturing giants that anchor South Carolina's position as a major leader in the aerospace and automotive industries.

Did You Know?

  • South Carolina is the largest producer of **peaches** in the US east of the Mississippi River.
  • The **Gullah Geechee** people on the Sea Islands have preserved a unique African-influenced culture for centuries.
  • The **Sabal Palm** on the flag became an iconic symbol after its wood was used to build forts that repelled British cannonballs.
