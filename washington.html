Overview

Washington is a Pacific Northwest powerhouse defined by its evergreen forests and volcanic peaks. As a global hub for technology, aerospace, and outdoor adventure, it boasts a dynamic economy anchored by industry giants and stunning natural geography.

Historical Significance

Washington joined the Union on November 11, 1889, as the 42nd state. Named in honor of George Washington, it evolved from the Oregon Territory into a major industrial and maritime center, driven by its strategic ports and vast natural resources.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Mount Rainier:

An iconic active stratovolcano and the highest peak in the state, serving as a massive center for hikers, climbers, and nature lovers.

Space Needle:

Built for the 1962 World's Fair, this observation tower offers panoramic views of the Seattle skyline and Puget Sound, symbolizing local innovation.

Pike Place Market:

One of the oldest continuously operated public farmers' markets in the US, attracting millions with its local crafts and fresh Pacific seafood.

Did You Know?

  • Washington is the **only US state named after a President** and is the nation's top apple producer.
  • The **Grand Coulee Dam** is one of the largest concrete structures in the world and a massive hydro-power hub.
  • Seattle is the **birthplace of global icons** including Boeing, Microsoft, Starbucks, and legendary guitarist Jimi Hendrix.
