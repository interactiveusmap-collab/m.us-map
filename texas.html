Overview

Texas, the 'Lone Star State,' is the second-largest state in both area and population. A global leader in energy, aeronautics, and technology, it boasts a massive international border and a culture rooted in cowboy tradition and fierce independence.

Historical Significance

Texas joined the Union on December 29, 1845, as the 28th state. Uniquely, it was an independent republic for nearly a decade after winning its independence from Mexico in 1836, a legacy personified by the historic Battle of the Alamo.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Space Center Houston:

NASA's premier visitor center where guests can explore the past, present, and future of America's human spaceflight program.

The Oil Industry:

The absolute heart of US energy production, driving a massive economy that leads the nation in petroleum and natural gas output.

King Ranch:

One of the world's largest working ranches (825,000 acres), symbolizing the state's deep agricultural and cattle-raising heritage.

Did You Know?

  • The **State Capitol** in Austin is taller than the US Capitol building in Washington, D.C.
  • Texas has its own **separate power grid**, making it uniquely independent in the contiguous US.
  • Texas is the only state to have **six different national flags** fly over it throughout its history.
