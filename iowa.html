Overview

Iowa, the "Hawkeye State," is the food capital of the world and a quintessential American heartland. Nestled between the Mississippi and Missouri rivers, it is an agricultural titan and a leading center for insurance and renewable energy.

Historical Significance

Iowa officially joined the Union on December 28, 1846, as the 29th state. It evolved from Native American homelands into the cornerstone of the nation's food supply and a pivotal state in the American political landscape.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Iowa State Capitol:

A stunning 19th-century masterpiece in Des Moines, famous for its magnificent gold-leaf dome.

Amana Colonies:

Seven historic villages established in 1855, preserved as a National Historic Landmark.

Agricultural Powerhouse:

Iowa consistently ranks as the nation's top producer of corn, pork, and eggs.

Did You Know?

  • Iowa is the only state whose east and west borders are formed entirely by major rivers.
  • The state generates over 60% of its electricity from massive wind turbine farms.
  • The first computer (ABC) was invented at Iowa State University between 1937 and 1942.
