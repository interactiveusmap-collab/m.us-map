Overview

South Dakota, the 'Mount Rushmore State,' is a majestic land of the Great Plains and the rugged Black Hills. Known for its iconic national monuments, it offers a rich blend of frontier spirit and breathtaking natural beauty.

Historical Significance

South Dakota officially joined the Union on November 2, 1889, as the 40th state. Its identity is rooted in the cultures of the Great Sioux Nation and the transformative legacy of the Black Hills Gold Rush.

Top Cities & Hubs

1

Sioux Falls

Population: ~195,000

The state's largest city and a massive regional hub for finance and healthcare, famous for its namesake waterfalls on the Big Sioux River.

2

Rapid City

Population: ~75,000

The 'Gateway to the Black Hills,' serving as the primary tourist and historical center for the western part of the state.

3

Aberdeen

Population: ~28,000

A major agricultural and healthcare center in the northeast, home to Northern State University and a vibrant community spirit.

4

Brookings

Population: ~24,000

Home to South Dakota State University and a flourishing center for research, engineering, and high-tech manufacturing.

5

Pierre

Population: ~14,000

The state capital, situated on the Missouri River, known for its historic charm and its role as the seat of South Dakota's government.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Mount Rushmore:

The colossal granite sculpture in the Black Hills that honors four legendary US presidents and American history.

Badlands National Park:

A world-renowned landscape of eroded pinnacles and spires that contains one of the planet's richest fossil beds.

Crazy Horse Memorial:

A massive under-construction mountain carving that pays tribute to the Lakota warrior and tribal heritage.

Did You Know?

  • The **Black Hills** are among the oldest geological formations in North America.
  • The **Corn Palace** in Mitchell is the only palace of its kind, decorated with murals made of corn.
  • South Dakota has **more miles of shoreline** than Florida, thanks to the massive Oahe Dam and Missouri River.
