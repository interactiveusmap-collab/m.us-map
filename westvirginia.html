Overview

West Virginia is a ruggedly beautiful state situated entirely within the Appalachian Range. Known as the 'Mountain State,' it is world-renowned for its dramatically forested landscapes, deep coal mining heritage, and elite outdoor sports like whitewater rafting and climbing.

Historical Significance

West Virginia joined the Union on June 20, 1863, as the 35th state, becoming the only state born from secession from a Confederate state during the Civil War. Its history is intertwined with the American labor movement and the industrial power of the coal industry.

Top Cities & Hubs

1

Charleston

Population: ~48,000

The state capital and commercial hub, beautifully located at the confluence of the Elk and Kanawha Rivers, serving as the heart of government and industry.

2

Huntington

Population: ~46,000

A historic Ohio River port city and home to Marshall University, celebrated for its vibrant downtown and its role as a regional healthcare center.

3

Morgantown

Population: ~30,000

Home to West Virginia University, this energetic college town is a primary driver of the state's technology, education, and research sectors.

4

Parkersburg

Population: ~29,000

A historic river city with a deep oil and gas heritage, serving as a vital gateway to the state's northern chemical and manufacturing corridor.

5

Wheeling

Population: ~26,000

The state's original capital, famous for its Victorian architecture and its legacy as a major transportation hub along the historic National Road.

Key Landmarks & Economy

New River Gorge:

One of the newest US National Parks, featuring an iconic bridge and world-class whitewater rafting amidst spectacular Appalachian scenery.

The Greenbrier:

A legendary luxury resort that has hosted 28 US Presidents and famously concealed a secret Cold War-era Congressional fallout bunker.

Blue Ridge Mountains:

Iconic mountain range providing the backdrop for the state's rugged identity and immortalized in the song 'Take Me Home, Country Roads.'

Did You Know?

  • West Virginia is the **only state positioned entirely within the Appalachian Mountains**.
  • The **First Mother's Day** service was held in Grafton, West Virginia, at Andrews Methodist Church in 1908.
  • The **New River Gorge Bridge** was once the world's highest bridge and remains a global site for base jumping on Bridge Day.
