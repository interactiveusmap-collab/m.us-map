Overview

Florida is a unique peninsula in the southeasternmost U.S., world-renowned for its tropical climate and over 1,300 miles of coastline. It serves as a global hub for tourism, international trade, and groundbreaking space exploration.

Historical Significance

Florida officially joined the Union on March 3, 1845, as the 27th state. Originally a Spanish colony found in 1513, it houses St. Augustine, the oldest continuously inhabited European-established settlement in the contiguous United States.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Kennedy Space Center:

The primary launch site for NASA's human spaceflight, located on Merritt Island.

The Everglades:

A unique subtropical wilderness providing a vital habitat for endangered species like the Florida panther.

Tourism Powerhouse:

Home to world-famous theme parks that drive the state's massive service-based economy.

Did You Know?

  • Florida is the only place on Earth where alligators and crocodiles coexist in the wild.
  • The state produces about 70% of the citrus fruit supply for the entire country.
  • No matter where you are in Florida, you are never more than 60 miles from the ocean.
