Overview

Vermont, the 'Green Mountain State,' is a New England jewel famous for its forested natural beauty and artisanal charm. As the second-least-populous state, it is a world leader in sustainable agriculture and premium maple syrup.

Historical Significance

Vermont officially joined the Union on March 4, 1791, as the 14th state and the first admitted after the original 13 colonies. It existed as an independent republic for 14 years and was the first state to partially abolish slavery in its constitution.

Top Cities & Hubs

1

Burlington

Population: ~45,000

The state's largest city, beautifully situated on Lake Champlain, celebrated for its vibrant arts scene, progressive culture, and the University of Vermont.

2

South Burlington

Population: ~20,000

A major commercial and tech hub within the Burlington metro area, featuring the state's largest airport and a thriving international business community.

3

Rutland

Population: ~16,000

Historically known as the 'Marble City,' Rutland is a key center for central Vermont commerce and a premier destination for mountain recreation.

4

Barre

Population: ~9,000

Recognized as the 'Granite Capital of the World,' Barre is famous for its massive quarries and the high-quality stone used in monuments worldwide.

5

Montpelier

Population: ~8,000

The state capital and the smallest capital city in the US, adored for its historic architecture, community focus, and golden-domed State House.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Maple Syrup Production:

Vermont is the undisputed leader in US maple syrup production, a cornerstone of its agricultural identity and heritage.

Shelburne Museum:

A world-renowned museum of art and Americana, featuring historic buildings and the famous Ticonderoga steamboat.

Premier Ski Resorts:

Home to legendary alpine destinations like Stowe and Killington, driving a massive tourism and outdoor sport economy.

Did You Know?

  • Vermont is the only New England state that **does not border the Atlantic Ocean**.
  • The state leads the nation in **microbreweries per capita**, with a world-famous craft beer scene.
  • Until 1996, Vermont was the only US state without a **single Walmart** within its borders.
