Overview

Ohio, the 'Birthplace of Aviation,' is a midwestern powerhouse with a soul forged in industry and innovation. From the bustling government and insurance hubs of Columbus to the historic riverfront of Cincinnati, Ohio serves as a critical bridge between the East Coast and the Heartland.

Historical Significance

Entering the Union on March 1, 1803, as the 17th state, Ohio's strategic location along the Ohio River made it a foundational gateway for westward expansion and a cornerstone of American industrial and political history.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Rock & Roll Hall of Fame:

An architectural marvel in Cleveland that honors the influential artists and history of rock music.

Wright-Patterson Air Force Base:

A massive military installation in Dayton that serves as the heart of US Air Force research and development.

National Museum of the US Air Force:

The world's oldest and largest military aviation museum, showcasing Ohio's legendary ties to flight.

Did You Know?

  • Ohio has produced **eight US Presidents**, more than any other state except Virginia.
  • Ohio's flag is the **only non-rectangular** state flag in the United States (it's a burgee).
  • The **first professional baseball team**, the Cincinnati Red Stockings, was established in Ohio in 1869.
