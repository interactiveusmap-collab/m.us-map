Overview

Tennessee, the 'Volunteer State,' is a Southeastern crown jewel defined by its rich musical heritage and diverse landscapes. From the peaks of the Great Smoky Mountains to the Mississippi River, it serves as a global hub for music, healthcare, and higher education.

Historical Significance

Tennessee joined the Union on June 1, 1796, as the 16th state. Famous for its citizens' prominent role in the War of 1812, it has been a focal point for American history, serving as a pivotal Civil War battlefield and the birthplace of world-changing musical genres.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Great Smoky Mountains:

The most visited national park in the US, celebrated for its staggering biodiversity and ancient, misty mountain landscapes.

Graceland:

The legendary former home of Elvis Presley in Memphis, serving as a global monument to the massive influence of rock 'n' roll.

Music Row:

Nashville's historic district, the absolute heart of the world's country music industry featuring legendary studios and venues.

Did You Know?

  • Tennessee is bordered by **eight states**, tying for the most neighbors of any US state.
  • The **Lost Sea** in Sweetwater is the largest underground lake in the United States.
  • Nashville's **Parthenon** is the only full-scale replica of the Greek original, complete with a massive statue of Athena.
