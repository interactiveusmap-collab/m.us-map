Overview

Kentucky, the "Bluegrass State," is a mesmerizing blend of rolling limestone hills, world-renowned horse culture, and deep-rooted Southern charm. Best known as the global birthplace of bourbon and home to the legendary Kentucky Derby.

Historical Significance

Kentucky officially joined the Union on June 1, 1792, as the 15th state. Originally part of Virginia, it became the first U.S. state west of the Appalachian Mountains, serving as a critical frontier gateway.

Key Landmarks & Economy

Churchill Downs:

The legendary racetrack in Louisville, home of the Kentucky Derby, the oldest major American sporting event.

Mammoth Cave:

The world's longest known cave system, a massive subterranean wonder attracting visitors globally.

Bourbon & Tech Ag:

Kentucky produces 95% of the world's bourbon whiskey while being a leader in automotive production.

Did You Know?

  • Kentucky is the birthplace of Abraham Lincoln, the 16th President, born in a log cabin near Hodgenville.
  • State is home to the world's largest gold collection, stored at the US Bullion Depository in Fort Knox.
  • Kentucky is the only U.S. state to have a continuous border with seven different states.
